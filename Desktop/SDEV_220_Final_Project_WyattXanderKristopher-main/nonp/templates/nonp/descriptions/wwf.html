The World Wildlife Fund (WWF) is a non-profit organization dedicated to protecting and conserving the natural world. The organization was founded in 1961 and operates in more than 100 countries around the world. The WWF's mission is to conserve nature and reduce the most pressing threats to the diversity of life on Earth.

The WWF's work is focused on several key areas, including wildlife conservation, habitat protection, and climate change. The organization works to protect endangered species and their habitats, including elephants, tigers, and pandas. The WWF also works to address the impacts of climate change, which threatens wildlife and natural habitats around the world. The organization's programs and initiatives are designed to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, promote sustainable energy and resource use, and build resilience to the impacts of climate change.

In addition to its conservation work, the WWF also works with governments, businesses, and communities around the world to promote sustainable development and responsible stewardship of natural resources. The organization works to promote sustainable fishing practices, reduce deforestation and habitat loss, and promote the use of renewable energy sources. Through its education and outreach programs, the WWF also works to raise awareness about the importance of conserving nature and the threats facing wildlife and natural habitats.
