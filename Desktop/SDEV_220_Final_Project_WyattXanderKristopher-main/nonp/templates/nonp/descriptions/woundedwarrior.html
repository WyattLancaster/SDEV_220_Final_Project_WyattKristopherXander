The Wounded Warrior Project (WWP) is a non-profit organization that provides a range of programs and services for injured veterans and their families. The organization was founded in 2003 and is headquartered in Jacksonville, Florida. The WWP's mission is to honor and empower wounded veterans by providing programs and services that meet their physical, mental, and emotional needs.

The WWP offers a variety of programs and services for wounded veterans and their families. These include mental and physical health and wellness programs, career and education services, and support for families and caregivers. The organization's goal is to provide wounded veterans with the tools and resources they need to achieve their goals and live full and productive lives. The WWP also advocates for policies and legislation that support injured veterans and their families.

The Wounded Warrior Project has helped thousands of wounded veterans since its founding. The organization's programs and services have helped veterans to overcome physical and emotional challenges, find meaningful employment and education opportunities, and build strong support networks. The WWP is committed to providing wounded veterans with the resources and support they need to achieve their goals and live full and productive lives.
