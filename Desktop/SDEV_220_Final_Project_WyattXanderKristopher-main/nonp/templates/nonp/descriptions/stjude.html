St. Jude Children's Hospital is a leading pediatric treatment and research facility dedicated to curing catastrophic diseases, primarily childhood cancers. Founded in 1962 by the late entertainer Danny Thomas, St. Jude's mission is to advance cures and means of prevention for pediatric catastrophic diseases through research and treatment. The hospital is located in Memphis, Tennessee and treats children from all 50 states and around the world, regardless of their family's ability to pay.

St. Jude Children's Hospital is unique in that it not only provides cutting-edge treatment to its patients, but it also conducts groundbreaking research in the fight against childhood diseases. The hospital's research efforts have led to some of the most important advances in pediatric cancer treatment, including the development of life-saving chemotherapy protocols and the identification of genetic risk factors for certain types of childhood cancers.

St. Jude also offers a range of support services to its patients and families, including education, counseling, and family housing. The hospital's commitment to providing compassionate care and hope to its patients and families is reflected in its motto, "Finding cures. Saving children."
