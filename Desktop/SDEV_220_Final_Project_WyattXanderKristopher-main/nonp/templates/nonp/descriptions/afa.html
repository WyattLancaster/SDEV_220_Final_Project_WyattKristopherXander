The Alzheimer's Foundation of America (AFA) is a non-profit organization that aims to provide support, services, and education to individuals with Alzheimer's disease and related dementias, as well as to their families and caregivers. Founded in 2002, the AFA is dedicated to promoting optimal care and quality of life for individuals affected by Alzheimer's disease. The foundation's vision is to ensure that individuals with Alzheimer's disease receive compassionate care, support, and resources, and that they are treated with dignity and respect.

The AFA offers a range of programs and services to help individuals with Alzheimer's disease and their families. These include a national toll-free helpline, educational conferences and workshops, a virtual memory screening program, a medication assistance program, and support groups for individuals with Alzheimer's disease and their caregivers. In addition, the AFA provides grants to support research into Alzheimer's disease and related dementias.

The AFA also advocates for policies and legislation that support individuals with Alzheimer's disease and their families. The foundation works with policymakers at the local, state, and national levels to promote Alzheimer's disease awareness and to improve access to quality care and support services. Through its efforts, the AFA is working to ensure that individuals with Alzheimer's disease and their families receive the care and support they need to live with dignity and quality of life.
